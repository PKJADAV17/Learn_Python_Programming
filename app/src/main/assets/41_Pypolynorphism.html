Python Polymorphism

Polymorphism is a fundamental concept in object-oriented programming (OOP) and is one of the four pillars of OOP, along with encapsulation, inheritance, and abstraction. The term "polymorphism" comes from Greek and means "many forms." In the context of programming, polymorphism allows objects of different types to be treated as objects of a common type.

In Python, polymorphism is often achieved through two mechanisms: method overloading and method overriding.
